How much did you pay in Gift Aid donations during the year?

  • When you give money to a charity or to a registered Community Amateur Sports Club (CASC) through Gift Aid, the charity or CASC can reclaim the tax that you’ve paid on that money.
  • You must have paid at least the same amount of tax that the charity or CASC is going to reclaim, or you could end up with a bill for the difference.
  • The amount you donate is treated as the amount after basic rate (20%) tax. The charity or CASC can claim 25p for each £1 you donate.
  • To work out the maximum you can donate through Gift Aid, you can multiply the tax you’ll pay by 4.

For example, if Jane is going to pay £100 tax this year, she can donate £400 to charity or a CASC through Gift Aid. The charity or CASC will claim back 400 x 25p, which is £100.

For more information on Gift Aid payments to charities, including how to claim Higher Rate tax paid on Gift Aid donations, visit www.gov.uk/donating-to-charity

You can find out more about registering a CASC here www.gov.uk/register-a-community-amateur-sports-club
